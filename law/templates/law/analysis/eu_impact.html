Introduction

{% blocktrans %} The Laws of a country are probably the written documents with most impact on the people's life, as they constitute the rules on which they live in a country. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} The European Union (EU) is today an important legislative body in creating laws that affect all EU state members and it has been claimed that the European elections are the most important elections so far. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} Here we quantify this claim. Our assumption is that european elections are important when the EU law has an impact on people's life, which is quantifiable by its impact on national laws. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} With this in mind, we posed the question: what is the impact of the european law in the portuguese law? {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Main result" %}

{% blocktrans %} To answer this, we picked all documents published in the Series I of "Diário da República", counted how many cited at least one european law, and divided that number by the total number of created laws, for each year, which gives us the percentage of portuguese laws that, at some level, are depending on a EU law. {% trans "Discussion" %}

{% blocktrans %} As expected, we observe a steady increase from 1986 (year Portugal joined EU), and today 14% of all portuguese laws cite at least one european law. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} Arguably unexpected is the change in sloop since ~2006, which gave rise to an increase of 10% in 7 years (4% to 14% from 2006 to 2013). This seems to indicate that not only European legislation is having more influence on the portuguese law, but also that this increase has been faster since 2006. {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Conclusion" %}

{% blocktrans %} Here, we measured quantitatively the impact of the european law in the portuguese law and we have shown that Europe legislation has a strong impact in portuguese law. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} Furthermore, we have shown that the change of impact increased since 2006, and today about 14% of all portuguese laws cite an european law at least once. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} This seems to suggest that European elections are not only important, but are also becoming more important as they have more impact on the rules that govern people's life. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} It remains to be studied if this effect is similar in other european states. {% endblocktrans %}
