Introduction

Laws of a country are probably the written documents with most impact on the people's life, as they constitute the rules on which they live in a country. Elections are in democracy an important bridge between people's will (what rules they want) and the implementation of their will (what rules they have).

In a joint effort by european states that believe in a set of common rules, the european union was formed and it is argued that today it is an important legislative body with a concrete impact to the life of european citizens.

Because the European law supersede the national law but is not, by itself, a national law, states must implement european decisions in their law. What most of us don't know is how much this happens. Thus, here we pose the question: what is the impact of the european law in the portuguese law?

Discussion

As expected, we observe a steady increase from 1986 (year Portugal joined EU) until today.

Arguably unexpected is a change in the sloop since ~2006, which gave rise to an increase of 10% in 7 years (4% to 14% from 2006 to 2013). This seems to indicate that not only European legislation is having more influence on portuguese law, but also that this increase is faster since 2006.

Conclusion

Here we quantitatively measured the impact of the european law in the portuguese law and we have shown that Europe legislation has a strong impact in portuguese law.

Furthermore, we have shown that the change of impact increased since 2006, and today about 14% of all portuguese laws cite at least once an european law.

This seems to suggest that European elections are not only important, but are also becoming more important as they impact more the rules that govern people's life.

It remains to be studied if this effect is similar to other european states.
