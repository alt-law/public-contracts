{% trans "Introduction" %}

{% blocktrans %} The Laws of a country are probably the written documents with most impact on the people's life, as they constitute the rules on which they live in a country. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} The European Union (EU) is today an important legislative body in creating laws that affect all EU state members and it has been claimed that the European elections are the most important elections so far. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} Here we try to quantify this. Our assumption is that european elections are important when the EU law has an impact on people's life, which is quantifiable by its impact on national laws. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} With this in mind, we posed the question: what is the impact of the european law in the portuguese law? {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Main result" %}

{% blocktrans %} To answer this, we picked all documents published in the Series I of "Diário da República", counted how many cited at least one european law, and divided that number by the total number of created laws, for each year, which gives us the percentage of portuguese laws that, at some level, are depending on a EU law. {% trans "Discussion" %}

{% blocktrans %} As expected, we observe a steady increase from 1986 (year Portugal joined EU), and 14% of all portuguese laws created in 2013 cited at least one european law. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} From 1985 to 2005 (20 years), there was a consistent increase to ~7%. We observe that there was a change in sloop since ~2005, which gave rise to an increase of ~7% in ~10 years, or double the sloop. {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Conclusion" %}

{% blocktrans %} Here, we measured quantitatively the impact of the european law in the portuguese law and we have shown that Europe legislation has a strong impact in portuguese law: in 2013 about 14% of all portuguese laws cited an european law at least once. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} Furthermore, we have shown that the velocity of this impact doubled since 2004: in 10 years (2003-2013) the percentage of laws citing european law raised as much as in the 20 years before (1985-2005). {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} This seems to support the idea that the European elections are not only important, but they are also becoming more important as they have more impact on the rules that govern people's life. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} It remains to be studied if this effect is similar in other european states. {% endblocktrans %}
